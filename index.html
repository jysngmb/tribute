Emilio Aguinaldo

1st President of the Philippines

Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy was a Filipino revolutionary, statesman, and military leader who is the youngest president of the Philippines (1899–1901)

    Emilio Aguinaldo's Early Life

  • 1869 - Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy is born on March 22 in Cavite el Viejo (now Kawit), Cavite, Philippines. He is the seventh of eight children to Carlos Aguinaldo and Trinidad Famy. Raised Catholic, he grows up in a well-to-do family. His father serves as the town’s gobernadorcillo.
  • 1878 - Aguinaldo’s father, Carlos, dies, leaving his mother, Trinidad, to manage the family’s affairs. At a young age, Emilio begins to understand the complexities of running a household and managing family lands.
  • 1882 - A cholera outbreak interrupts Aguinaldo’s studies at the Colegio de San Juan de Letran in Manila. He returns home to Cavite to help his mother with the family’s agricultural endeavors.
  • 1896 - Aguinaldo is elected as the first gobernadorcillo capitan municipal of Cavite el Viejo. His leadership becomes increasingly important as tensions with Spanish authorities rise. He joins the Katipunan, a revolutionary society aiming to liberate the Philippines from Spanish rule.
  • 1898 - Declared President of the Revolutionary Government. The Philippine-American War begins following the Spanish-American War.
  • 1901 - Captured by American forces on March 23, ending the Philippine-American War.
